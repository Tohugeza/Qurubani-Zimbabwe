Your Qurbani journey

What Happens Between Now and Eid

Your donation sets a chain of events in motion across two countries. Here is every step from your payment today to the moment fresh meat reaches a family in Zimbabwe on Eid day. Exact dates for 2026 will be confirmed closer to Eid — the steps follow the same pattern as 2025.

Step 1 · Now

Your Qurbani is Confirmed

Your payment has been received and your Qurbani is secured. You will receive a confirmation email with full details. The team in the UK and Zimbabwe are notified and the planning begins immediately. JazakAllah Khayran — your generosity makes everything that follows possible.

Step 2 · Weeks before Eid

Contact Farmers for Availability

The Zimbabwe team contacts local subsistence farmers in the Beatrice area to confirm goat availability and quality. Sourcing from local farmers supports the rural economy and ensures the animals are healthy and locally raised. The number of goats sourced is matched exactly to donations received.

Step 3 · Before Eid

Purchase Confirmed · Authorities Notified

The goats are purchased from the farmer in Beatrice and arrangements are finalised. In line with local cultural and political customs, community chiefs and local authorities are formally notified of the upcoming Qurbani. This step ensures the full cooperation and trust of the community on the day.

Step 4 · Early Eid Morning

The Sheikh Travels to the Farm

Early on Eid morning the Sheikh from Gilston Masjid is collected and transported to the farm in Beatrice where the goats are held. The sacrifice is performed in accordance with Islamic guidelines, with the intention (niyyah) made on behalf of each donor and the loved ones they have named. It takes approximately one hour to complete.

Step 5 · Eid Morning

Meat Preparation

A dedicated team handles the full preparation of the meat. This includes careful cleaning, portioning into 3kg packages suitable for a family, and ensuring everything is properly prepared and ready for distribution. Each portion is thoughtfully sized to provide a meaningful and generous share for those who receive it.

Step 6 · Eid Afternoon

Transportation to Gilston Masjid

The prepared meat is transported from the farm in Beatrice to Gilston Masjid. The road to the Masjid is remote and rough — its isolation is part of why this community is so often overlooked by other Qurbani programmes. This year, because of your generosity, they will not be overlooked.

Step 7 · Eid Afternoon

Distribution · Photos Sent to Every Donor

Fresh meat is distributed to families from Gilston Masjid — which is hosting Qurbani for only the second time in its history. For many of these families this is the only time all year they receive fresh meat. Photos of the distribution are taken and sent directly to every donor so you can see exactly where your Qurbani went and whose Eid you made possible.

Confirmation email

You will receive a full confirmation of your Qurbani by email shortly. Keep this as your record of participation.

Photos after Eid

We will send you photos of your Qurbani and the distribution on the ground so you can see the impact of your giving first-hand.
